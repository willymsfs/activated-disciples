Chapter 5: Going All In

Mapping the Journey of a Covenant Relationship with Christ

From Intellectual Assent to Active Pursuit

The good news is that the call to follow Christ is as fresh and challenging today as it was for the apostles. However, it's easy to fall into the trap of responding only intellectually. Discipleship must go beyond study and result in an active life of prayer, service, and evangelization. The first step is to acknowledge God's love and accept His invitation to walk with Him.

The author uses the analogy of a spousal relationship to describe this process, which unfolds in three key stages. This framework helps us identify where we are in our relationship with God and where we need to go.

The Three Stages of a Spousal Relationship with God

1. Courtship (Dating )

This is the initial phase, often driven by our own needs and a sense of emptiness. We "cry out" to God, seeking something more. The focus is very personal, a "me and Jesus" experience. It's a time of discovery and falling in love.

2. Commitment (Engagement)

The relationship deepens. We realize we want to go beyond casual dating and make a formal, public commitment. This involves submitting to the Church (Christ's family) for preparation and guidance, moving from a private feeling to a public decision.

3. Communion (Marriage)

This is the consummation of the relationship. We enter into a full covenant communion with God through the Sacraments of Initiation (Baptism, Confirmation, Eucharist). We become one with Christ and His Body, the Church, and are equipped for a lifelong mission.

Deep Dive: The Courtship Phase

In this stage, a person often develops a voracious appetite for all things God‚Äîreading books, listening to talks, and seeking out fellow believers. It's like the excitement of a new romance. While beautiful, it's important to understand that this is the beginning, not the end. Courtship is not marriage.

The "Sinner's Prayer": A Starting Line

The author introduces the idea of an initial prayer of acceptance. This isn't a "magic formula" for salvation but a way to solidify a decision and mark a spiritual starting line. It's like accepting an offer for a first date‚Äîpurposeful and moving, but non-binding. The relationship will be formally sealed in the covenant later.

Prayer for a New Believer: "Dear Lord Jesus, I thank you that you love me... I know that I have sinned... I want to repent... As I submit myself to your covenant family, the Church, help me to walk humbly and discover my place in your family. Amen."

Deep Dive: The Commitment Phase (The Catechumenate)

Just as an engagement is a more serious commitment than dating, this phase represents a formal decision to devote one's life to Christ. For those entering the Catholic Church, this is the RCIA (Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults) process. It's not just a class; it's a critical period of formation where one is formally joined to the household of Christ and prepared for full communion. It's serious business.

Commitment involves being embraced by the Church, the family of God.

Deep Dive: The Communion Phase (The Sacraments )

This is the "wedding day." The journey culminates at the Easter Vigil, where catechumens receive the Sacraments of Initiation. This is a life-changing transformation:

  • Baptism: We are washed of sin, become a "new creature," and are bonded to the Body of Christ.
  • Confirmation: We receive the full outpouring of the Holy Spirit, empowering us to be authentic witnesses. This is a key to becoming an "activated" disciple.
  • Eucharist: We are admitted to the "marriage supper of the Lamb," receiving the Body and Blood of Christ. This is the source and summit of Christian life, where we experience total mutual self-giving.

Through this process, we enter into **theosis** or "divinization"‚Äîsharing in the very life of God.

Core Concept: To Be "All In"

When we say "yes" to Christ's invitation, we are like a poker player pushing all their chips to the center of the table and declaring, "I'm all in." It's a statement of total confidence and commitment. It's echoing the "yes" of the Blessed Virgin Mary, who didn't just appease an angel‚Äîshe reconfigured her entire life around her "yes."

Becoming like Christ isn't a matter of just believing; it requires massive amounts of time with Him in prayer, study, and adoration. The world doesn't need part-time disciples; it needs intentional, activated disciples who will have an impact on the world.

Chapter 5 Summary & Reflection

This chapter provides a clear and practical map for the journey of faith, using the analogy of a developing relationship. We move from the initial excitement of Courtship, to the formal decision of Commitment, and finally to the life-altering reality of covenant Communion. This process, sealed in the Sacraments, calls for a total, "all-in" response, where we reconfigure our entire lives to participate in the mission of Jesus Christ as members of His Body, the Church.
