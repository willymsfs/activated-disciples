Taking Your Faith to the Next Level

The Journey in Review: From Fan to Follower

We have traveled a great distance together. We began by asking a fundamental question: Are you a fan or a follower? We discovered that true discipleship is not about passive admiration but active, intentional participation in a relationship with Jesus Christ.

This journey took us deep into the heart of salvation history, revealing that the call to be a disciple is an ancient one, rooted in the call to **imitate God** (Chapter 2 ). We learned that this journey begins not with our initiative, but with God's divine choice—He is the Rabbi who **chooses us** first (Chapter 3). We mapped out the **process of discipleship**, seeing it as a structured journey of learning and commitment (Chapter 4) that mirrors the stages of a spousal relationship: Courtship, Commitment, and Communion (Chapter 5). Finally, we equipped ourselves with the practical **disciplines** (Chapter 6), the daily rhythm (Chapter 7), and the core message—the **kerygma** (Chapter 8)—needed to live as activated disciples in the world.

The Forty-Day Challenge: Putting It All into Practice

Throughout the book, Jeff Cavins alludes to a forty-day challenge. This is the practical application of everything we have learned. The purpose of a forty-day period is significant in Scripture—it represents a time of testing, transformation, and preparation (e.g., Noah and the flood, Moses on Mount Sinai, Jesus in the desert).

Now, it is your turn. The end of this book is not a conclusion, but a commencement. It is an invitation to embark on your own forty-day adventure to solidify the habits of an activated disciple.

Your 40-day journey of transformation starts today.

Your Commitment for the Next 40 Days

This challenge is a focused effort to reconfigure your life around a daily, structured, and disciplined relationship with Jesus. Here is what it entails:

Daily Prayer

Commit to a specific time and place for prayer each day, using the Lectio Divina method to converse with God through Scripture.

Intentional Living

Practice the discipline of kavanah. Be constantly aware of God's presence. Talk to Him throughout your day and sanctify your daily activities.

Sacramental Life

Deepen your appreciation for the Sacraments. If possible, attend a daily Mass, go to Confession, and consciously unite your life with the Church's liturgical calendar.

Ready to Share

Keep the kerygma in your heart. Pray for opportunities to share the reason for your hope with others in a natural, loving way.

A Final Word of Encouragement

This journey will take change—it is all-consuming—but it is worth it. You are not just trying to become a better person; you are responding to the call to become like Jesus, engaged in building His kingdom and changing the world.

Remember the promise from the beginning: If you commit to the change that Jesus can bring to your life, you will be a different person. You will be more knowledgeable, yes, but more importantly, you will be more like Jesus.

Where Do You Go From Here?

This educational series is complete, but your journey is just beginning. Use the tools and concepts from these chapters as your guide. Mark your calendar for the next 40 days. Find a friend to take the challenge with you for accountability. Most importantly, trust in the one who chose you for this adventure.

Final Questions for Reflection:

  • Am I truly ready to accept the 40-day challenge? What is the biggest obstacle I foresee, and how can I plan to overcome it?
  • Who is one person I can invite to join me or hold me accountable on this journey?
  • Looking back at all the chapters, what is the single most important concept that has impacted me and that I want to carry into this challenge?
