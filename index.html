I. Introduction: The Call to a Deeper Faith

Many of us are like enthusiastic fans in the grandstands of faith—we know the stats, wear the colors, and cheer loudly. But are we on the field? This guide begins with a provocative question: Are you a fan or a follower? It's a journey to bridge the gap between passive belief and an active, life-altering relationship with Jesus Christ.

The journey of discipleship is a direct response to a personal invitation from Jesus.

II. The Core Concepts of Discipleship

Concept 1: Fan vs. Follower

The foundational distinction is understanding the difference between being a "fan" of Jesus and a "follower." A fan admires from a distance, consuming information without application. A follower engages in a transformative relationship that involves putting faith into practice.

The Fan (The Spectator )

  • Knows the information, loves the idea
  • Faith is a hobby or interest
  • Risks boredom and stagnation

The Follower (The Player)

  • Puts knowledge into practice
  • Faith is a transformative lifestyle
  • Experiences joy and purpose

Concepts 2 & 3: The Divine Blueprint

Discipleship is an ancient call to **imitate God** ("Be holy, for I am holy"). This journey begins not with our choice, but with God's. He is the Divine Rabbi who **chooses us** first, believing we have the potential to become like Him. Our "yes" is a response to His prior "yes" to us.

"You did not choose me, but I chose you." (John 15:16)

Concepts 4 & 5: The Process and Disciplines

Becoming a disciple is a structured process, not an accident. It involves taking on the "yoke" of Christ—His entire worldview—and committing to daily disciplines that foster growth. The primary tool for this is the ancient practice of Lectio Divina.

1

Lectio (Reading)

Read the text slowly, listening for a word or phrase that resonates.

2

Meditatio (Meditation)

Chew on that word. See how it connects to your life.

3

Oratio (Prayer)

Converse with God about what you've read. Share your heart.

4

Contemplatio (Contemplation)

Rest in God's presence, moving beyond words.

Concepts 6 & 7: Daily Life and Mission

The goal is to be "covered in the dust of the Rabbi"—to follow Jesus so closely that our entire life is shaped by Him. This unified life then becomes our mission. We share the core Gospel message, the **Kerygma**, not as a formula, but as the natural overflow of a transformed heart.

1

God loves you and has a plan.

2

Sin ruins that plan.

3

Christ died to save you.

4

Repent and believe.

5

Be baptized in the Holy Spirit.

6

Abide in the Church.

7

Go make disciples.

III. Conclusion: The 40-Day Challenge

Learning is the prelude to living. The journey of the activated disciple culminates in a commitment to practice. This 40-day challenge is your opportunity to intentionally forge the habits that will transform your faith from a noun into a verb.

Your 40-day journey of transformation starts today.

Now is the time to be transformed—now is the time to be an activated disciple. Are you ready to take your faith to the next level?
