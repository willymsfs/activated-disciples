The Activated Disciple

A Complete Guide to the Core Concepts

Based on the work of Jeff Cavins

Introduction: The Call to a Deeper Faith

Many of us who identify as Christians are, in reality, more like enthusiastic fans than active followers. We know the statistics, we wear the team colors on Sunday, and we might even have a bumper sticker on our car. We admire Jesus from a distance, but our daily lives remain largely unchanged. This guide begins with this provocative question to diagnose a common spiritual condition: a faith that is heavy on information but light on transformation.

The journey of discipleship is a direct response to a personal invitation from Jesus.

The core problem this guide seeks to solve is the disconnect between what we say we believe and how we actually live. It's a call to move beyond a mere intellectual agreement with Jesus and to enter into a dynamic, life-altering relationship with Him.

The Core Concepts of Discipleship

Concept 1: Fan vs. Follower

The foundational distinction is understanding the difference between being a "fan" of Jesus and a "follower." A fan admires from a distance, consuming information without application. A follower engages in a transformative relationship that involves putting faith into practice.

Example: The Photography Enthusiast

Jeff Cavins describes his obsession with photography. He bought all the gear and read every magazine, but never actually took a picture. He was a fan of photography, not a photographer. Similarly, one can know all about the Faith but never actually live it.

Concept 2: Discipleship as Imitation ("Be Holy" )

The blueprint for discipleship is ancient, found in God's command to Israel: "Be holy, for I am holy." The core of discipleship is imitation. We are called to conform our lives to the pattern of God's life, which He perfectly models in the person of Jesus Christ.

Concept 3: Discipleship is Being Chosen

The relationship begins with the master's choice. The rabbi chooses the disciple he believes has the potential to become like him. Jesus, the Divine Rabbi, states this clearly: "You did not choose me, but I chose you" (John 15:16). This truth counters the modern feeling of rejection; the Creator of the universe has chosen you.

Concept 4: Taking on the "Yoke" of the Rabbi

The invitation to follow Jesus is paired with the command, "Take my yoke upon you." A rabbi's "yoke" was his entire worldview. To take on the yoke is to make a 100% commitment to adopt the rabbi's way of life as your own. It is a covenant, not a casual agreement.

Concept 5: The Disciplines of a Disciple

Growth requires intentional, daily disciplines. The primary tool is Lectio Divina (Divine Reading), a method for turning Scripture into a conversation with God. This is supported by practical habits: picking a consistent time, place, and plan for prayer.

Concept 6: Covered in the Dust of the Rabbi

This powerful image encapsulates the goal of daily discipleship: to follow Jesus so closely that the "dust" of His life and teachings covers you. This requires shaping your entire day around Him, creating a rhythm of prayer and constant awareness of His presence (kavanah).

Concept 7: Sharing the Kerygma

The final step is mission. Evangelization is not about complex theology but about sharing the simple, powerful, core message of the Gospel—the kerygma. Your changed life is the "event" that opens the door to explaining the reason for your hope and inviting others to take their own next step.

Conclusion: The 40-Day Challenge

This guide is not just for your information, but for your transformation. The end of learning is the beginning of doing. The book invites you to embark on a 40-day challenge—a focused period to solidify the habits of an activated disciple.

Now is the time to be transformed—now is the time to be an activated disciple. Are you ready to take your faith to the next level?

Your Commitment for the Next 40 Days:

  • Daily Prayer: Commit to a specific time and place for prayer using Lectio Divina.
  • Intentional Living: Practice a constant awareness of God's presence throughout your day.
  • Sacramental Life: Deepen your appreciation for the Sacraments.
  • Ready to Share: Keep the kerygma in your heart and pray for opportunities to share your hope.
